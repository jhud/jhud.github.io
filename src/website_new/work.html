What I do

I think the world has enough scooter, ad-selling, and food delivery conglomerates. I've stayed as a freelance developer because I want independent, imaginative, and worthwhile projects to be able to access the same development resources as large tech companies.

For new projects, I typically develop products and services from the concept to public release, and advise on technical matters.

For existing projects, I can use my broad experience to jump into a team at short notice, to provide additional development muscle or specialised expertise.

I don't work with recruiters; you can talk with me directly.
