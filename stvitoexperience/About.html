What is the ST. VITO Experience?

HOW DOES IT WORK?

ST. VITO Experience is a multimedia project which fuses music, visuals, technology, audience interaction, dance and stilts theater. We perform in front of, amongst, and with, audiences in clubs, festivals, and theatres.

We literally embody music: we have developed unique screen costumes together with Jasmin Lepore, the fashion designer of Sasha Waltz, to transform performers' and audiences’ entire skin surfaces into touchable musical instruments and light shows.

Our dancers’ screen costumes function as MIDI inputs into the live production equipment of any music producers, giving them endless options for incorporating the audience’s touch input into their performance, making our show a unique, real-time sound and light experience. Our dancers are transformed into otherworldly, glowing, magical creatures which respond to touch and music.

The project is produced by Alma Edelstein and James Hudson: James has a long professional background in software development and media arts, and Alma is an established producer, dancer, and choreographer.

CONCEPTUAL ART vs. LIGHT SHOWS:

Our interactive performances vary from minimalistic, poetic dance-theater, to striking and extravagant happenings. The number of performers can be adapted to any performance space.

TECHNO meets ANALOG ANDROIDS

OUR COOPERATION WITH MUSIC PRODUCERS AND DJ´S

We literally embody music: our sensors are full MIDI controllers, which can enrich the sounds of any electronic music producer. By touching our androids' skins, the audience can co-create the music in real-time; their skins literally become the producers´ control surfaces.

WHO ARE the ST. VITO Experience?

We are a group of professional dancers, musicians, actors, choreographers, performers, software engineers, technicians, costume designers, film makers and producers. All of us are bound by a shared vision: to give the audience the main role in an interactive experience and share the excitement and playfulness of public performance. We create a context for people to become curious and sensitive, offering them new ways of communicating through touch and movement. By interacting with us physically, the audience becomes part of the creative process. Our goal is to manifest a more optimistic, peaceful future, where people are more in touch with others.
